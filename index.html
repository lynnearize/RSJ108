How to Pitch a News Story

Assembly time: 30 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Do you want your writing to reach a larger audience? Learn how to pitch to publishers and get your work out there with this simple guide.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Email address
  • News story or content idea
  • Time Management Skills
  • Courage!

  1. Find a news story or idea that you’re motivated to write.

    Follow websites, and social media accounts, that have information about events or people related to your topic. For example, if you want to write about museums, follow their social media and websites.

    Keep an eye/ear out! The best way to find stories is by paying attention to what the people around you have to say, and looking out for any posters/ads that might give you information on an interesting event, organization or person.

  2. Find a publisher that will be interested in your story or idea.

    Search for news outlets, magazines, and publishers that publish stories on your topic.

    Examples: “Museum News,” “Museum Magazines,” etc.

    University news outlets will be more open to publishing your work as a student, so it's a good place to start!

  3. Do your research.

    Look at the style, tone, length of articles, and more. Adjust each story to match the style of the publication..

    4. Newspaper stand in Barcelona, Spain.

  4. Draft the email.

    Get to the point. Publishers and editors are busy, and are probably sifting through multiple pitches a day. You want to make sure that they know who you are, and what your idea is in the first two sentences.

    Hi [editor/publisher name],

    My name is [first and last name], and I’m a [student writer/journalism student/ etc.]. I’d like to pitch a story about [specific story idea].



    Include a suggested headline, word count and deadline. Share your credentials. Mention where else you’ve been published and links to writing samples. Even if it’s on your personal website, make sure to include it!

  5. Follow up!

    If you don’t hear from them in a couple of days make sure to follow up. Don’t be overbearing, and if they don’t respond after a second follow up, it’s best to move on.
